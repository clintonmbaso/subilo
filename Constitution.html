ARTICLE 1: PRELIQUISITE

Section i: Name and Logo

a. Name: The name of this group shall be “Subilo Investment”, hereafter, abbreviated as “S.I” which will be the name and initials used in publicity materials, correspondence and representations of the group. In this document, S.I may be referred to as the “group” or “team”.

b. Logo: the logo for the group shall be as given below. This logo shall be used on all official publications of the group.


Section ii: Organization

a. The committee members of S.I shall make up the Executive Board of the group, hereafter, referred to as the ‘Executive Board’ which shall be composed of the chairpersons, the secretaries, and the treasurers.


Section iii: Terminology

i. Shareout: this shall refer to the total amount of money that each member shall receive at the end of the circle.

ii. Circle: this shall refer to the full period within which each investment is to be completed; i.e. 6 months.

iii. Investment: this shall refer to the total amount of money one has placed in the group for accumulation.

iv. Rate: this shall refer to the percentage at which the invested amount is to be calculated. i.e. 20% accumulation rate.

v. Recommender / Guarantor: this shall refer to the person that introduces a member to the group.

vi. Registration fee: this shall refer to the fixed amount of money that each member shall have to pay once every circle for them to qualify to be part of S.I.

vii. Share plan: this shall refer to the number of shares one decides to invest into within the range of 1 – 50 shares.

viii. Shares: this shall refer to the investment calculations in the ratio 1:1000; i.e. one share for each ZMW 1000.00 invested monthly.

ARTICLE 2: PURPOSE OF THE GROUP

Section i: Overall Group (S.I)

a. To provide a platform for its members to invest for a good shareout

b. To provide an opportunity for members to borrow funds, and

c. To promote economic opportunities and the sharing of business ideas


Section ii: Executive Board

a. The purpose of the Executive Board is to plan for the investments, organize activities, keep the group informed, run the affairs of the group, insure that all members are in line with the code of conduct, and ensure that all members are given their correctly calculated shares at the end each circle.
ARTICLE 3: MEMBERSHIP

Section i: Membership Policy

a. Our membership shall not be limited by gender, race, ability or tribe. We shall endeavor to accommodate all that shall be willing to abide by this constitution.


Section ii: Process of attaining Membership

a. All aspiring applicants shall be given this document to go through thoroughly in order for them to decide either to accept the terms and conditions herein and in the by-laws or not.

b. Each member will have to sign a consent form to signify acknowledgment and acceptance of all the terms and condition herein and in the by-laws.


Section viii: Revoking Membership

a. Membership may be revoked by the Executive Board by way of letter, if a member:

i. decides they want to leave in line with Section (vii) of Article (7).
ii. does not abide by this constitution
iii. dishonors Section (i)a of Article (4).
ARTICLE 4: COMMITMENT

Section i: Commitment

a. It is the duty of every member of this group to be fully committed and involved in all the activities of the group. Being an investment group, it is mandatory for each member to take part in the growth of the investment of the group.

b. Every member is mandated to borrow up to 80% of the shareout so that every member takes part in the growth of the funds.
ARTICLE 5: MEETINGS AND PROCEDURES

Section i: Meetings

a. The group shall have monthly meetings for group financial analysis either physically or virtually as per directive by the Executive Board.

b. Monthly meetings shall be held every 30th day of the month at a time and mode of meeting that the Executive Board will have to be communicating each time.

c. All members are supposed to attend all meetings. A member that knows may not make it or will be late for a particular meeting, should inform the Executive Board through the office of the secretary at least 3 days before the meeting day clearing stating concrete reasons; unless it is an emergency.

d. Members that abscond or come late for any given meeting without having communicated through the aforementioned office shall be subjected to discipline through a fine that is stated in the by-laws.

Section ii: Procedures
a. A majority of the group shall constitute a quorum to transact any business.

b. Each member shall be entitled to one vote and the vote of the majority shall constitute an affirmative move of the group in cases of group decisions.
ARTICLE 6: OFFICERS OF THE GROUP AND ELECTIONS

Section i: Officers

a. The group shall have the following officers:

i. Chairpersons: 2 - Male or Female
ii. Secretaries: 2 - Male or Female
iii. Treasurers: 2 - Male or Female

b. No office shall be left vacant. If there be any officer that is not available to provide their services, the office of the secretary shall assume the responsibility.

c. The chairperson reserves the right to appoint as a delegate any one whom he/she deems fit to assume a vacant position until the time the position is filled in.


Section ii: Responsibilities

a.CHAIRPERSON
The role of the chairperson at its most basic is to preside over meetings of the group, but shall include the under listed responsibilities:
i.Chairing meetings
ii.Planning meetings and developing the agenda in collaboration with the secretary.
iii.Providing leadership and ensuring the effective operation of the group.
iv.Ensuring that funds are correctly accounted for and safeguarded.
v.Ensuring that decisions made at meetings are implemented.
vi.Acting as a spokesperson for the group.
vii.Overseeing Treasury.

b.VICE CHAIRPERSON
The role of the Vice chairpersons at its most basic is to preside over meetings of the group, when the chairperson is absent but shall include the under listed responsibilities:
i.Standing in for the chairperson if he or she is unavailable.
ii.Assisting the chairperson with matters regarding meetings.
iii.Dealing with specific tasks or issues as defined by the membership (for example, chairing ad-hoc meetings or dealing with personal matters of the group).
iv.Overseeing Secretariat.
v.Ensuring adherence to set rules of the group.
vi.Following up and encouraging members that fall behind with their savings and repayments.

c.SECRETARY
The role of the secretary at its most basic is to keep accurate minutes of meetings of the group, but shall include the under listed responsibilities:
i.Helping the chairperson to plan meetings.
ii.Organizing the logistics for meetings.
iii.Taking and distributing minutes.
iv.Keeping Attendance registers.
v.Dealing with group’s communication.
vi.Keeping Member records (Physical and Postal Addresses, E-mail Addresses, Phone Numbers, Recommender’s details, Passport Size Photos, Next of Kin details etc.).

d.VICE SECRETARY
The role of the Vice Secretary at its most basic is to perform the functions of the secretary in his or her absence, but shall include the under listed responsibilities:
i.Assisting the secretary in all secretarial duties.
ii.Keeping all records for the group.

e.TREASURER
The main role of the treasurer is at its most basic to maintain a financial overview of the group, but shall include the under listed responsibilities:
i.Looking after the finances.
ii.Overseeing transaction postings.
iii.Preparing and presenting financial reports to the members.
iv.Ensuring adequacy of the group’s resources.
v.Ensuring that appropriate accounting procedures and controls are in place.
vi.Liaising with members about financial matters.
vii.Advising on the financial implications of any transactions.
viii.Presenting the accounts report at the end of the cycle.
ix.Responding to queries from members regarding financial related issues.

f.VICE TREASURER
The main role of the Vice Treasurer is at its most basic to perform the functions of the Treasurer in his or her absence, but shall include the under listed responsibilities:
i.Assisting the Treasurer in all financial duties.
ii.Money counting and financial record keeping.
iii.Cash box key holding or Account managing.


Section iii: Tenure of Office

a.All officers are to save for 5 years.
ARTICLE 7: CODE OF CONDUCT

Section i: General

a. The registration fee that shall be stated in the by-laws shall be paid at the beginning of each circle.

b. The full orbit of the circle shall be 6 months.

c. The last month shall not be calculated for interest for it shall be the month for payments.

d. The minimum number of shares each member shall be allowed to invest in shall be in the range of 1 to 50.


Section ii: Rates and benefits

a. The group shall invest the money that each member invests at the rate of 20%.

b. The profit margin for every share plan shall be calculated at 68%.


Section iii: Saving

a. Dates of savings shall be between 20th and 30th of each month.

b. Saving every month according to one’s preferred share plan is mandatory for all. Failure to save within the given time or doing so after the 30th of the month will attract a fine as stipulated in the by-laws.

c. If a member decides they want to upgrade their share plan, they should do so in the first three months, and they should backdate the savings from the first month based on the difference between their current share plan and the intended, then add on interest as if they had borrowed the upgrade amount.


Section iv: Lending / Borrowing

a. All registered members of the group are obligated to borrow from the group.

b. Borrowing should be on the 30th of every month.

c. If money has not been fully loaned out by the 30th, the interest shall be shared equally after ensuring that everyone has a loan for that particular month. If someone decides to use their stipulated full share value, they will take it as a loan.

d. Whatever money is given to any member should be approved by the committee.

e. In the last two months, the money will be shared under group equal sharing of the interest unless one decides to get their value as a loan.

f. The borrowed amount shall not exceed the take home amount based on the share plan one decides to adopt in that particular circle.

g. Any member that intends to borrow an amount that is more than there shareout value, the guarantor’s account will stand in as surety: the recommender should have invested into a share plan whose total cash out amount when added to the borrower’s total cash out amount should amount to or more than the amount to borrowed.


Section v: Returns

a. Each loan borrowed shall be repaid before the end of the circle.

b. The minimum amount of return is the 20% interest of the borrowed amount.

c. In instances where a member fails to return the minimum amount, the recommender shall have to pay the amount in question.

d. If a member fails to return the borrowed amount with the required interest within the given time, the Executive Board shall be obliged to fine the member as stipulated in the by-laws.


Section vi: Communication

a. All communications regarding the group, should be done within the respective WhatsApp groups.

b. If any member has a concern, they have to communicate to the relevant personnel according as per responsibility stipulated in Section (ii) of Article 6.


Section vii: Withdrawing

a. No one shall be allowed to withdraw from the group before the end of the circle.

b. If one decides to withdraw from the group, they should inform the executive board through the office of the secretary 3 months before the new circle begins.
ARTICLE 8: CREATION OF BY-LAWS

Section i: By-law Policy
a. All by-laws shall be established in correspondence to the constitution. Any law that goes against the constitution shall not be ratified.

Section ii: Process
a. The Executive Board shall reserve the rights to establish, approve, adopt and enact all by-laws.
b. All by-laws shall be signed by the chairperson and secretary.
ARTICLE 9: RULES ON AMENDMENTS AND INTERPRETATIONS

Section i: Amendments
a. All amendments to the constitution may be proposed and seconded by two thirds vote of the Executive Board on any article, or Section, or Part.

b. Members that observe areas of this constitution that need amendments shall forward their observations to the executive board who shall take it to a vote after analysis as per stipulation of Section (i)a of Article 9 above.


Section ii: Ratification
a. The constitution shall be established and deemed ratified by a vote of the majority of the Executive Board as described in Section (ii) of Article (6).


Section iii: Interpretations
a. Any questions raised concerning the interpretation of this document shall be determined and answered by the Executive Board.
